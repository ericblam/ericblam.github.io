Chasse Cape (Gold)

Commence in Closed or Contact Position

Notes to Man and Lady

1 Beat value: 1, 1, 1, 1/2, 1/2, 1, 1, 1, 1/2, 1/2, 1, 1, 1, 1/2, 1/2, 1, 1, 1, 1, 1, 3 beats for holding the Spanish Line
2 The free arm is extended to side, just above shoulder level on 20 (the Spanish Line), or raised high above head as explained in the Spanish Lines
3 A Press Line may be danced in place of the Spanish Line, the front foot extended further forward with more weight and a stronger flexing of the front knee, the front heel well off the floor

Ending 3: Lady's Spin to Right

Man Foot Position Alignment Turn Foot-
work		 Lead & Shape Count
1 Close LF to RF Facing centre 3/8 to L B of both feet Return L arm to normal position 1
2.3 2 Sur Place, RL " No turn B or BF Raise L arm and circle L hand very slightly clockwise, then lower to normal position 2.&
4  RF to side " " B or BF Achieve PP Shape 3

Note

 On 2, Man may turn Lady to R and release hold. Return to normal hold on completion of Lady's turn

Following Figures to all Endings

Gold Any syllabus figure commenced with feet together, facing centre

Note to Man and Lady

 A shorter version of the Chasse Cape may be danced as follows:
  Dance 1-8, omit 9-18, then continue with 19-21

Development (Outside Turn)

A useful way of turning from facing centre to facing DW in PP is to dance steps 1-3 of the Chasse Cape and then step to side in PP, moving along LOD. This could be danced after 1-10 of the Chasse Cape
